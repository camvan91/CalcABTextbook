	\paragraph{Example 1:} Find the integral of $h(x)=x^2\sqrt{x^3+1}$ over [0,1]
	First, let's find a good phrase to set for $u$. $x^3+1$ looks good:
	\begin{gather*}
		x=0\mapsto u=1\\
		x=1\mapsto u=2\\
	\end{gather*}
	Next, let's find $du$:
	\begin{gather*}
	u=x^3+1
	du=x^2\, dx
	\end{gather*}

	Plugging in:
	\[
	\int_{1}^{2}\sqrt{u}\, du
	\]
	Which is simple from here on out.\\
